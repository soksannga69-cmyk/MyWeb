Common Grammar Mistakes

When learning tenses, students often confuse:

  • Present Simple vs Present Continuous: Simple is for habits, Continuous is for now
  • Past Simple vs Present Perfect: Simple is finished in the past, Perfect is before now
  • Will vs Going to: Will for decisions, Going to for plans
  • For vs Since: For shows duration, Since shows the starting point
