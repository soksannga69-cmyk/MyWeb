English Grammar Review

Study important English tenses with structure and examples

Present Simple

Structure:

Subject + Verb(s/es)

Use:

Used for habits, facts, and daily routines.

Examples:

I play football every Sunday.

She studies English at school.

Present Continuous

Structure:

Subject + am / is / are + Verb-ing

Use:

Used for actions happening now.

Examples:

I am reading a book now.

They are playing in the yard.

Present Perfect

Structure:

Subject + has / have + V3

Use:

Used for actions that happened before now.

Examples:

I have finished my homework.

She has visited Japan.

Past Simple

Structure:

Subject + Verb2

Use:

Used for completed actions in the past.

Examples:

I watched TV yesterday.

He went to school last Monday.

Past Continuous

Structure:

Subject + was / were + Verb-ing

Use:

Used for actions happening at a specific time in the past.

Examples:

I was studying at 8 PM.

They were playing football yesterday afternoon.

Future Simple

Structure:

Subject + will + Verb1

Use:

Used for future plans, predictions, or decisions.

Examples:

I will study tomorrow.

She will travel next month.

Future Continuous

Structure:

Subject + will be + Verb-ing

Use:

Used for actions that will be happening in the future.

Examples:

I will be reading tonight.

They will be working at 9 AM tomorrow.

Future Perfect

Structure:

Subject + will have + V3

Use:

Used for actions completed before a future time.

Examples:

I will have finished my work by 6 PM.

She will have arrived before noon.
